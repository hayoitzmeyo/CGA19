How It Works 

If you're curious to learn about how our algorithms work or about the accuracy of our data structures, look down below for more information about each of our assessment topics!

Summary

Georisk takes in your address and feeds it into multiple processing functions in the backend. Your address is never saved on our end for safety purposes.
Our powerful AI and machine learning models—trained on decades of historical and real-time data use geocoding to analyze your location for a wide range of risks, including wildfire, burglary, flood, earthquake, and landslide.
If you are curious about how the algorithms work and how we formulate our data structures, we wrote papers on every one of our assessment topics. They will be linked down below with some general data regarding their contents.
The Basics

This paper covers the basics of how this website works. How we convert your address into workable coordinates, the structure of our website, how we formulate responses, the way our different algorithms are structured, normalization methods, and much more. 

Resource: Georisk - Website and Coordinate Integration

Wildfire Risk

This paper covers the data behind our wildfire risk algorithm. We use dozens of parameters spanning from the government sponsored ArcGIS project to our own neural network trained off of historical fire density maps. Learn how we combine this data to create an accurate representation of the fire risk of a certain place over time. 

Resource: Georisk - Wildfire Risk Analysis

Flooding Risk

This paper goes deep into our proven system to calculating flooding risk. We use metrics such as distance to bodies of water, elevation, density maps, weather pattern predictions and more to calculate flooding risk. 

Resource: Georisk - Flooding Risk Analysis

Earthquake Risk

This paper covers how we use extensive, advanced algorithms to calculate earthquake risk for a specific address. Trained on historical data, we use models centralized around short-term earthquake prediction as well as other information like soil classification, seismic hazard information and more to calculate an accurate earthquake risk.

Resource: Georisk - Earthquake Risk Analysis

Landslide Risk

In this paper, we explore how different metrics like slope, aspect, rain intensity, bedrock structure and more affect the landslide risk of a certain address. We combine these different metrics in order to produce an accurate analysis of the landslide risk. 

Resource: Georisk - Landslide Risk Analysis

Home Crime Risk

Home crime risk might sound abstract and difficult to calculate, but we use a logic-driven method in order to find the risk of burglary and more for a certain address. This includes using GIS layering data, historical crime rates, law enforcement vicinities, and more. 

Resource: Georisk - Home Crime Risk Analysis
